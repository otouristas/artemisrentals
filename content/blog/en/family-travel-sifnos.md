## Car over scooter for most families

Shade, space for beach kit, and fewer logistics when someone needs an abrupt nap return to the hotel. Scooters are fun for couples; families usually sleep better in a hatchback.

## What to tell Artemis

Mention passenger count, child seat needs, and luggage (ferry bags add up). A 5-seater compact is enough for island distances if you pack smart.

## Pace the island

Do one major beach or village cluster per day. Park early at Platis Gialos in August. Use Apollonia evenings for gelato walks without driving.

Compare [cars](/en/cars), read [things to do](/en/sifnos-guide/things-to-do), then [send dates](/en/book), no prepayment required to enquire.

## Beaches that work with children

Vathi offers shallow entry and space to spread out, easy parking and tavernas for lunch without a long walk from the car. Platis Gialos has more amenities but August crowds; arrive before 10 a.m. or after 4 p.m. Kamares suits arrival-day swims when kids are tired from the ferry.

Chrissopigi requires a short walk from parking, beautiful but less ideal with a heavy stroller. Save it for older kids or carrier-wearing toddlers.

## Child seats and vehicle fit

Request child seat type and age when you enquire, Artemis prepares allocation before pickup at **Apollonia** or **Kamares on request**. A 5-seater compact fits two adults, two children, and soft bags if you avoid hard suitcases on the rear floor.

Automatic transmission reduces hill stress leaving beaches with a sleeping child in back, worth the peak-season premium on Peugeot 208 Auto or Micra Auto.

## Sample family day rhythm

Morning beach until hunger strikes, car nap on the drive back toward Apollonia, pool or quiet hour at the hotel, evening gelato walk without driving again. One major outing beats three rushed stops, aligns with our [3-day itinerary](/en/blog/3-day-sifnos-itinerary) pacing.
