## Apollonia, capital energy

Apollonia (also called Stavri in local speech for the central spine) is where Sifnos concentrates nightlife, shops, bakeries and late tables. Whitewashed lanes climb and fork; after 21:00 in summer the centre hums. It is the natural place to **collect rental keys**, drop beach gear at a hotel, and walk to dinner without moving the car again.

**Do in Apollonia**
- Evening volta through the main pedestrian lanes
- Souvenir and jewellery browsing
- Late dessert and people-watching
- Practical errands: ATMs, pharmacies, mini-markets

**Parking reality:** Prefer hotel parking or edges of the centre; do not block narrow lanes. Once parked for the evening, walk.

## Artemonas, neoclassical calm

![Artemonas](/images/sifnos/villages/artemonas.webp "Artemonas")

A few minutes above / beside Apollonia, Artemonas feels greener and more residential: mansions, courtyards, and quieter nights. Ideal if you want capital access without sleeping above a bar.

**Do in Artemonas**
- Golden-hour architecture walk
- Breakfast with a view
- Base for travellers who like calm mornings

## Kastro as an easy evening add-on

![Kastro](/images/sifnos/villages/kastro.webp "Kastro")

From the Apollonia area, Kastro is a short drive for sunset walls and Aegean wind. Park outside the tight medieval core and walk in.
