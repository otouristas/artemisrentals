## What makes Sifnos different

Sifnos sits in the western Cyclades with a personality of its own: **serious cooking**, living **pottery** traditions, a dense network of **footpaths**, and beaches that reward a little travel from the port. It is quieter than the headline party islands, yet lively enough in Apollonia that evenings never feel empty.

Distances look tiny on a map. In practice, summer heat, beach gear, and limited bus frequency make **your own wheels** the default for stays of three nights or more.

## Geography in one minute

- **Kamares (west):** main ferry port and a full-service bay beach.
- **Apollonia & Artemonas (centre):** capital energy + neoclassical neighbour.
- **Kastro (east):** medieval cliff village and views.
- **South coast:** Platis Gialos, Faros, Chrysopigi, classic beach loop.
- **West bay:** Vathi, sheltered sand and pottery heritage.
- **North:** Herronisos and Vroulidia, quieter, longer drives.

| Stay | What fits |
| --- | --- |

| 4–5 days | Classic loop: two beach days, villages, Chrysopigi, food |
| 7 days | Add north coast, hikes, pottery workshop, optional boat day |

## Seasons

- **May–June & September–October:** Sweet spot, swimable seas, easier parking, softer light.
- **July–August:** Hottest and busiest; start beach days early; book popular tavernas.
- **Shoulder / winter:** Quieter island rhythm; confirm ferry and rental availability.
